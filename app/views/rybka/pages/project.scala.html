The Space project opened the Space for the mankind, the Human Genome project unveiled the code of life... Our Rybka (The “little Fish”) Project is much more modest. It is destined to clarify memory consolidation mechanisms and open animal genetic engineering for citizen scientists.

The Rybka project is a research on molecular foundations of the memory and mechanism of long-term memory formation, storage and retrieval. Unlike similar existing projects we will study dynamics of learning and memory in the freely living animal without any surgical intervention. Such an animal will be able to learn and explore the environment in most natural way. This approach enables to reach many unknown aspects of consolidation and memory formation because the animal’s behaviour is left intact.

For this purpose we chose Zebrafish (Danio Rerio) as an experimental animal because

it is clever enough for learning and some other forms of complex behaviour
there is a transparent breed of the zebrafish called Casper. We can modify its genome by adding glowing proteins (GFP) and observe internal processes in its brain using ordinary high-resolution cameras
the fish is relatively easy for transfection (adding transgenic DNA to the fertilized egg)
its genome is fully sequenced and publicly available

Similar technique was used in the cancer research. The GFP was liked to promoter of the gene expressed in vessels. We cans see fluorescence of the growing vessels (green) and the tumor (red) .

The project will be run with broad involvement of the citizen scientists. The biohackers will train themselves in transfection technologies. We will also study learning and synaptic plasticity. We will open-source all protocols, software and wetware and write a guide to let everybody reproduce the experiment at a low cost and adapt our methods for other research problems.
We hope that the fish will become a good model for studying genetic engineering and promoting science.
